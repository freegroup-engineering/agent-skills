# Worked examples

Before/after rewrites. Each one names which principles were deployed and why. Use these as templates to adapt, not to ship as-is.

---

## 1. Landing page hero — generic → Cialdini-informed

### Before (generic, bad)
> **Personalized AI Videos for Your Kids**
> Create custom cartoons in minutes. Upload a photo, pick a theme, and watch your child come alive on screen. Join 10,000+ happy parents today!
> [Start Your Free Trial]

What's wrong: vague social proof ("10,000+ happy parents" — unspecific and possibly not true), feature-first, no pre-suasion, no tribe, clichéd language ("come alive on screen"), CTA asks for commitment before any value delivered.

### After (Cialdini-informed)

> **What if the song your toddler can't stop humming was about them?**
>
> We built this for our son Liev, the week potty training nearly broke us. It's a 90-second cartoon — his name, his face, his milestone — and the first one's on us.
>
> *— Ronen & Eve, iniminimo*
>
> **[Make Liev's first video free → no card]**

Principles:
- **Pre-suasion** (hero question): puts the parent into imagining their own kid's voice/face before anything else.
- **Liking + unity**: parent-to-parent, named founders, specific struggle ("the week potty training nearly broke us"), identifiable tribe.
- **Reciprocity**: real first video free, no card. Compute cost is real, gift is real.
- **Authority (soft)**: "potty training" is a specific milestone frame; implies the team thinks in developmental terms.

Principles *not* used: social proof (deferred below the fold — too early to lean on here), scarcity (not real yet; would be faked).

## 2. Waitlist email — generic → improved

### Before

> Subject: You're on the waitlist!
>
> Hi there,
>
> Thanks for signing up! We'll let you know when iniminimo is ready. In the meantime, follow us on Twitter and Instagram.
>
> — The iniminimo team

What's wrong: wastes the privileged moment of high-interest. No commitment extraction. No unity. Asks for follow, gives nothing back. Reads as automated.

### After

> Subject: One question so your first video is about *them*, not just *a* toddler
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Eve here (co-founder, mom of a 2yo named Liev).
>
> Before we launch I'd love your advice on one thing: **what milestone is hardest for your toddler right now?** (Bedtime? Tooth-brushing? Getting dressed? Separation? Something we haven't thought of?)
>
> Just hit reply with a word or two. Whatever you send, your first video will be on that.
>
> Thanks — this stuff only works if it's real.
>
> Eve
> iniminimo

Principles:
- **Pre-suasion**: subject line primes the parent to think about *their* toddler specifically.
- **Unity (advice frame)**: "I'd love your advice" — collaborator mode, not customer mode.
- **Commitment**: naming the hardest milestone is a written micro-commitment to a problem iniminimo solves. Carries into onboarding.
- **Liking**: real person, real name, parent-to-parent.
- **Reciprocity (promised & delivered)**: "your first video will be on that" — gift is real and personalized.

Principle chosen *not* used: scarcity. No need here.

## 3. Referral program copy — generic → improved

### Before

> **Refer a friend, get $10!**
> Share your referral link and earn $10 for every friend who signs up.

What's wrong: transactional framing. Low amount. Triggers persuasion-knowledge — the reader knows they're being used as an acquisition channel. No unity. No tribe. No reason to feel good about sharing.

### After

> **The parents who should see this before their kid's 3rd birthday**
>
> If you've got a friend whose toddler is about to hit a big milestone — potty, pre-K, a new sibling — they'll get their first video free (same as you did). If they make a second, we'll credit you a third.
>
> You know the tribe. Send it where it lands.
> **[Copy your link]**

Principles:
- **Unity**: "the tribe," "the parents who should see this" — referrer and referee as identity-sharing.
- **Commitment (labelling)**: "You know the tribe" pre-supposes the reader is in the tribe and knows other members.
- **Reciprocity**: free first for the friend (same as referrer got), credit for the referrer *only if* the friend makes a real second video — aligns incentives honestly.
- **Social proof (implicit)**: "same as you did" reminds the referrer they went through it and valued it.

Why this beats the $10: the transactional version extracts a referral and depletes liking. The identity version builds liking in the act of sharing. Conversion may or may not be higher in the short term; lifetime value and brand affect almost certainly are.

## 4. Cold outreach — Ronen to Kling AI asking for credits

### Before (generic bad)

> Subject: Partnership Opportunity
>
> Hi Kling Team,
>
> My name is Ronen and I'm the founder of iniminimo, a fast-growing startup in the personalized video space. We'd love to explore a partnership with Kling AI.
>
> We have been impressed with your platform and believe we can drive significant value for both companies. Would you be open to a 30-minute call to discuss?
>
> Looking forward to hearing from you.
>
> Best,
> Ronen

What's wrong: nothing specific, nothing reciprocated, "fast-growing" is empty, 30-minute ask is too big a commitment, no privileged moment, triggers persuasion-knowledge immediately.

### After (Cialdini-informed)

> Subject: The "Liev's bath time" video we made with your API (and where we hit a ceiling)
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> Watched your [specific recent demo / model release / blog post] — the [specific technical detail] is what made me finally try it with our kid's face. This is the result: [15-second link].
>
> I'm Ronen, building iniminimo — personalized milestone videos for toddlers (potty training, new siblings, first day of school). 700 parents on the waitlist. We've generated ~400 test clips with Kling in the last month.
>
> We're hitting the rate-limit wall about 3 weeks before launch. Any chance of a credit bump or higher tier access for the launch month? Happy to share a case study on [specific technical detail you appreciate], or include Kling in our launch materials — whichever is useful to you (or neither — no pressure either way).
>
> Either way, appreciate what you're building.
>
> Ronen
> iniminimo

Principles:
- **Pre-suasion + liking**: the specific technical observation about *their* product as the first line.
- **Reciprocity**: real output already made (compute cost real), not "we'd love to build with you."
- **Authority (demonstrated)**: one sentence showing domain specifics (rate-limit ceiling, 400 clips, timing before launch).
- **Commitment (low-friction)**: asks for a specific bounded thing, with multiple yes-shapes to make it easy to engage.
- **Reactance reduction**: "or neither — no pressure either way" opt-out framing.
- **Unity (soft)**: "appreciate what you're building" — fellow-builder frame.

Why this works: the reader gets three things before the ask — a genuine observation about their work, a real result proving iniminimo can execute, and a specific usage profile that makes the ask legible. By the time the ask appears, it reads as reasonable, not opportunistic.

## 5. Onboarding first screen — generic → improved

### Before
> **Welcome to iniminimo!**
> Enter your email to get started.
> [Email field] [Continue]

### After
> **First — what milestone is hardest for your toddler right now?**
> *(We'll build your first video around whatever you pick. No signup needed yet.)*
>
> [ Potty training ]
> [ Brushing teeth ]
> [ Getting dressed ]
> [ Sleep / bedtime ]
> [ New sibling ]
> [ Something else ]

Principles:
- **Pre-suasion**: the first question you ask defines the frame for everything that follows.
- **Commitment**: naming the milestone is a micro-commitment to a problem.
- **Reciprocity**: "No signup needed yet" — you give first.
- **Unity**: the milestone list is itself a tribe signal (these are the real-life struggles this audience faces).

Delayed email capture is deliberate. Once the parent has invested even 15 seconds + one commitment, email-gate conversion climbs. Ask for the email *after* the first preview.

## 6. Founding-parents cohort copy

### Before (fake scarcity version — don't ship)
> **Hurry! Only 3 founding member spots left!**
> [Countdown: 02:34:17]

Problem: parents detect instantly, brand damaged.

### After (real scarcity)
> **Founding parents (500)** — open through [real date]
>
> Before iniminimo launches publicly, we're taking 500 founding parents who'll shape what gets built. In return:
>
> - **$X/month, locked forever** (launch price will be $2X)
> - A direct Slack line to the team
> - A vote on every new theme we release
> - Your kid's name on the "thank-you" screen of every launch video
>
> 237 / 500 claimed. [See who's in]
>
> [Become a founding parent]

Principles:
- **Scarcity (real)**: 500 is the actual cap. Lock-in is structural. "237 / 500" is live and genuine.
- **Social proof**: "See who's in" shows real people.
- **Reciprocity**: concrete founder-perks that cost iniminimo something.
- **Unity**: "founding parents," a tribe with a name.
- **Commitment (public)**: if the reader becomes a founding parent, their commitment is public and durable.

Why this survives scrutiny: every claim is structurally enforceable. A parent checking three months from now will find the cohort capped and the perks honored. That's the difference between scarcity as a tool and scarcity as a trick.

## 7. Internal ask — Freddie to Ronen

### Before
> Hey, I was thinking we should hire another agent for handling investor comms and I put together a whole doc with a proposed CLAUDE.md and tools and stuff, lmk what you think.

Problem: thinking-out-loud, high-friction ask, no privileged moment, forces Ronen to do the evaluation work.

### After
> Hey — proposed: a new agent (Rae) for investor comms. Scope: draft updates, prep for calls, chase replies. Would pull ~2–3 hrs/week of my current load.
>
> Short brief + CLAUDE.md draft: [link]. 3-minute read.
>
> **Yes / redirect / park for 2 weeks?**

Principles:
- **Pre-suasion**: the first line puts Ronen in the state "evaluating a defined proposal," not "wading through thinking."
- **Commitment (low-friction)**: three-option reply.
- **Authority (demonstrated)**: specific hour-load figure shows the case is made, not assumed.
- **Reactance reduction**: "park for 2 weeks" is a graceful no that doesn't feel like a rejection.
